title: "The UNIX Legacy of macOS"
---

The history of macOS and its evolution from UNIX traces back to the early days of computing. UNIX was developed in the late 1960s at Bell Labs by a team led by **Ken Thompson** and **Dennis Ritchie**. It was designed to be portable, scalable, and efficient, making it suitable for a wide range of hardware platforms.

During the 1970s and 1980s, UNIX gained popularity across academic, research, and commercial environments. Numerous UNIX variants emerged, each introducing unique features and design philosophies.

In the mid-1980s, Apple Computer, Inc. faced growing challenges with the classic Macintosh operating system. As personal computing demands increased, Apple sought a more stable and scalable foundation. In 1987, Apple partnered with AT&T to create **A/UX**, a UNIX System V–based operating system for Macintosh hardware. Despite its technical merits, A/UX failed to gain traction and was eventually discontinued.

Apple made another attempt in the late 1980s with an ambitious internal project known as **Pink**, led by **Jean-Louis Gassée**. The goal was to create a modern, object-oriented operating system capable of running on diverse hardware. However, organizational and technical difficulties ultimately led to the project’s cancellation.

A pivotal moment came in **1996**, when Apple acquired **NeXT**, the company founded by **Steve Jobs** after his departure from Apple. NeXT’s operating system, **NeXTSTEP**, was UNIX-based and highly regarded for its robustness and modern design. Apple used NeXTSTEP as the foundation for its next-generation operating system, initially released as **Mac OS X Server 1.0**. The first consumer version, **Mac OS X 10.0**, launched in **2001**.

Since then, macOS has undergone continuous evolution. In **2007**, **Mac OS X Leopard (10.5)** introduced **Cocoa**, a modern application framework based on Objective-C that simplified macOS development. Another major milestone arrived in **2016** with **macOS Sierra (10.12)**, which brought **Siri** to the desktop.

The macOS user interface has also evolved significantly. **macOS X Yosemite (10.10)** introduced a flatter, more modern visual style in **2014**, which was further refined in later releases such as **macOS Big Sur (11)**.

Today, macOS remains one of the most widely used operating systems in the world. Its **UNIX-based foundation** continues to provide a powerful, stable, and flexible platform for both developers and users.
